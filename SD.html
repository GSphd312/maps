What Does It All Mean?

Mean (Average)

The mean is the most common measure of central tendency. It's calculated by summing all the numbers in a dataset and then dividing by the count of those numbers. It gives you a single value that represents the 'center' of the data.

Standard Deviation (Spread)

Standard deviation (often abbreviated as SD or Ïƒ) measures how spread out the numbers in a dataset are from their mean. A small standard deviation means the data points are tightly clustered around the mean. A large standard deviation indicates that the data points are spread out over a wider range of values.
