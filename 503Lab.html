Public Health Data Analysis (Lab Assignments)

Question Sets 3, 5, 6, 7.1. Created by Dr. Brian Chen

Step into the role of a public health data analyst to tackle critical challenges using a simulated dataset, SPSS, and Microsoft 365 Copilot. Interpret for professional and lay audiences and reflect on AI across each stage.

CEPH C2 • C3 • C4 • C18 • C19 CIDI: Clarify → Ideate → Develop → Implement

Your Core Mission

Analyze the Urban Health & Environmental Exposures dataset to inform program planning and policy. Select a theme (e.g., obesity, environmental exposure, mental health, chronic disease) and build skills across four modules.

The CIDI Model

Each Question Set cycles through Clarify, Ideate, and Develop. In QS7.1 you complete Implement by creating a public-facing deliverable based on your cumulative analyses.

Assignment Progression & Workflow

QS3 (Descriptive Stats) QS5 (Relationships) QS6 (Group Comparisons) QS7.1 (Categorical + Synthesis)

At each stage: Analyze in SPSS → Replicate in Copilot → Interpret for two audiences → Reflect on AI

Simulated Dataset & Codebook

Work with the Urban Health & Environmental Exposures Survey (9,808 respondents across 15 neighborhoods). Review the codebook for variable definitions and scales before analyzing.

  • Data File: Urban Health & Environmental Exposures Data.xlsx
  • Codebook: Urban Health & Environmental Exposures Codebook.xlsx

Tools You’ll Need

Use SPSS for primary analysis and Microsoft 365 Copilot (UIS login) to replicate and reflect. Attach both the data and codebook to your Copilot chat.

Ethical Reminder

  • Verify AI outputs and cite limitations.
  • Note any bias, misinformation, or framing concerns.
  • Describe how you would mitigate issues in practice.

QS3 — Descriptive Statistics

Frequencies • Means • SD • Counts

Public Health Theme (100–150 words)

  • Choose a theme (e.g., obesity, air quality, mental health).
  • Ensure your theme can be explored with the dataset's variables.

Research Question #1

Formulate a descriptive statistics question related to your selected theme. This should focus on patterns, averages, or distributions in the data.

Variables & Data Collection (100–150 words)

  • Based on your theme and Research Question #1, identify relevant variables from the dataset.
  • Briefly describe an appropriate quantitative data collection method for gathering this data.

Findings & Interpretation

  • Run your analysis in SPSS, then interpret your results using your own words for both professional and lay audiences.

AI Reflection

  • Compare Copilot with SPSS, summarize their prompts, and note learning or ethical concerns.

QS5 — Relationships
